Introduction to Subspace Clustering

@Shawn Lu · May 11, 2018 · 3 min read

THE SUBSPACE CLUSTERING PROBLEM Modeling a collection of data points with a union of subspaces. Specifically, Given set of points $\{x_j\in\mathbb{R}^D\}_{j=1}^N$ Drawn from an unknown union of linear or affine subspaces $\{S_i\}_{i=1}^n$ of unknown dimendions $d_i=dim(S_i)$ The subspaces can be described as $S_i=\{x\in\mathbb R^D:x=\mu_i+U_iy\}$, i=1,...,n $y\in\mathbb R^{d_i}$ is a low-dimensional representation for point $x$ The goal of subspace clustering is to find the number of subspaces $n$, their dimensions $\{d_i\}_{i=1}^n$, the subspace bases $\{U_i\}_{i=1}^n$, the points $\{\mu_i\}_{i=1}^n$, and the segmentation of the points according to the subspaces.
